### 2. Google Expands Gemini Personal Intelligence to All US Users

**Google announced that Personal Intelligence is now available to all US users** across AI Mode in Search, the Gemini app, and Gemini in Chrome. Previously limited to paid subscribers, this feature allows Gemini to connect with Gmail, Photos, YouTube, and Search to provide personalized responses.

**Key Points:**
- Free-tier users can now access Personal Intelligence (previously paid-only)
- Opt-in feature: users control which apps to connect
- Gemini can answer complex questions using data from connected apps (travel plans, purchase history, etc.)
- Privacy-focused: Google doesn't train directly on personal data from Gmail/Photos
- Rolled out March 17, 2026

**Use Cases**: Finding tire specs from Photos, planning trips based on past travel memories, shopping recommendations matching previous purchases.

### 4. Supermicro Co-Founder Charged with Smuggling $2.5B in AI Chips to China

**Three individuals affiliated with Supermicro have been charged** with conspiring to smuggle advanced Nvidia AI chips into China in violation of US export controls. The scheme involved at least $2.5 billion in AI technology.

**Key Points:**
- Defendants: Wally Liaw (Supermicro co-founder), Steven Chang (sales manager), Willy Sun (contractor)
- Chips included Nvidia B200 and H200 GPUs banned for export to China since 2022
- Scheme: Servers shipped to Southeast Asia, repackaged, then smuggled into China
- Liaw and Sun arrested; Chang remains a fugitive
- Each faces up to 30 years in prison if convicted
- Supermicro stock plunged 25% on the news

**Context**: This highlights ongoing US-China tech competition and enforcement of AI chip export restrictions.
